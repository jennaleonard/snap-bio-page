Hi! My name is Jenna.

I'm an artist, musician, and visual designer. I recently decided to pivot into tech and hone my skills as a front-end developer.

Here are some recent projects

Relentless Sound

Relentless Sound is a website that was created for a fictional women-owned indie music label. I worked with a team of less than 10 people to create and launch the project in under two weeks. I was responsible for designing the logo, high fidelity mock-ups, style tile, and coding the CSS.

The Book Emporium

The Book Emporium is a fictional bookstore (featuring some of my favorite reads). I created the site on Wordpress and using a custom template and custom plugins.

Client Blog

I created this project for my coding bootcamp. The prompt was to make a client blog and practice using HTML and CSS. Additionally, I added navigation, high contrasting colors, and responsiveness to the site.

A bit about me

I grew up drawing, writing stories, and climbing trees in rural Michigan. Back when MySpace was popular, I taught myself how to code with basic HTML and CSS. I spent two years studying visual communications and graphic design before graduating high school. After that, I began working as a freelance designer.

I’m naturally drawn to solving puzzles, learning languages, and working with technology; but it wasn’t until I went through my coding bootcamp that I realized I could roll all of these traits into one profession.
